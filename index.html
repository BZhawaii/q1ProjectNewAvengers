New Avengers

Have you ever wondered why the Avengers had so many weaker heroes, like Black Widow and Hawkeye? The team is supposed to be fighting intergalactic threats and defending the planet from the most powerful villains. The results do speak for themselves. However, we have all wondered what the Avengers would be like if we could hand pick the heroes that were on the team. Well, this is your opportunity to do just that. On this page you will have the opportunity to name your team of Avengers and then to pick each of the individual heroes that will make up your awesome new team. Leader

Powers are one of the most integral aspects to any superhero's ability to be of use to a team. It is of utmost importance to have a few members of an Avengers team be very powerful. In the past Hulk, Thor, and Iron Man have held the mantle for the most powerful Avenger. The power's that the individual members have are one of the major factors in determining the strength of the foe that the team can handle.

Co-leaders

Intelligence is another aspect to be considered in the hero members of the team. While it isn't necessary to have multiple geniuses on the team, it helps to have a strong level of intelligence in the majority of the team. Having heroes with strength and low intelligence can have a negative impact on the overall effectiveness of the team. After all, having a team with five Hulks would not be that effective of a team. Although they would have immense power at their dispoasal, they might not be able to use it effectively.

Rank and File

Another important factor in determining the overall effectiveness of your Avengers is the selection of your leaders. The team leader must have intelligence, strategic abilities, and a command presence. After all, a leader can't be effective if he doesn't have the respect of his teammates. Another thing to consider is that, while you don't need multiple team leaders, a strong team will have strong secondary leaders. This is particularly effective when splitting up the team into multiple teams. Strong leadership will make the dividing of the teams much easier.
